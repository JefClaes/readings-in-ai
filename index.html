Readings in Artificial Intelligence

This is a curated guide to the foundational papers behind modern AI. It traces how the field arrived at its current state — from a single artificial neuron that could learn from examples, to systems capable of reasoning, generating images, and taking actions in the world.

The chapters are intended to be read in order. Each body of work directly motivates what follows. Papers were selected for conceptual novelty: those that introduced a genuinely new idea, not those that merely extended existing ones. Together they represent roughly 100 pages of reading.
